Biography

Gorne Kevlor was born in 1469 DR, the son of an unusual couple: Sir Aldren Kevlor, a human paladin from the Order of the Gauntlet, and Varka, chieftain of the orcish Moonhunters tribe. They met when Aldren was leading a mission to secure the High Road near Neverwinter and found a surprising ally in Varka. Over time, they fell in love and got married. It wasn't easy though. Higher ups in Neverwinter who were invested in Fort Defiance's progress saw the trust in orcs as naive and dangerous, and even Aldren's own order initially opposed their union.

Growing up, Gorne spent time both at Fort Defiance and in Moonhunter Valley. His father taught him swordsmanship and the paladin's valor, while his mother showed him how to hunt and to control the "Blood of Gruumsh," the powerful but very dangerous rage that exists within all orcs. She often reminded him that the one thing that distinguished Moonhunters from the savage orc was their ability to control this rage and not give in to it.

When Gorne was younger, a large gray wolf named Stormfang started appearing at important moments in his life, like during dangerous fights or lonely travels. It would vanish afterward, sometimes for months or even longer. The Moonhunter shamans said this was a sign of favor from Silvanus, the god of nature.

Despite facing prejudice due to his mixed heritage, Gorne's charismatic personality and ability to put anyone he spoke to at ease won over all who interacted with him. He was often described by people as having a charming and carefree personality, yet strict in his determination to uphold justice.

"Here, have this. It's not sour, it's good, just like me!" — Five-year-old Gorne, offering an apple to a visiting knight who, like most, didn’t initially trust the sight of a half-orc child

He dedicated himself to following the paladin's path; at age fifteen, he officially pledged himself to Tyr and became one of the few half-orc paladins along the Sword Coast. When he turned eighteen, Gorne met Penelope during a diplomatic summit in Neverwinter, a half-elf diplomat working under Lord Neverember (though secretly connected with the Harpers).

Gorne fell for her completely and without reservation.

He would later tell his parents that from their first real conversation in the Blacklake District, he knew she was the one. Within a week, they became a couple. Within a month, Gorne proposed to her under a full moon. Their quick romance raised eyebrows, but anyone who saw them together understood immediately; they simply belonged with each other.

Gorne was utterly devoted to Penelope, his eyes rarely leaving her when they were in the same room. Life seemed perfect as they built their future between the city and frontier... until tragedy struck. Just months after they married, Penelope was killed defending a village from a Cragmaw goblin raid while delivering diplomatic messages.

Upon hearing news of a goblin attack, Gorne immediately remembered Penelope's mission to deliver messages to that very village. His heart pounding with dread, he rode desperately on horseback, silently pleading for her safety. Arriving at the village, he was met by a nightmarish scene: buildings ablaze, the ground littered with victims of the Cragmaw goblins' brutal raid. Amidst the smoke and ruin, Gorne discovered Penelope's lifeless body, her diplomatic messages scattered beside her. Grief consumed him entirely, twisting swiftly into rage. Then and there, he denounced Tyr's call for justice and embraced Hoar's promise of vengeance, allowing the Blood of Gruumsh to surge unchecked through his veins. Now, with his divine magics empowered by Hoar's ruthless divine fury instead of Tyr's measured justice, he tracked and wiped out much of the Cragmaw tribe, not even sparing defenseless elders and infants.

His parents tried to stop him and help their son still his rage, but during the conflict, Gorne seriously wounded his father Aldren, permanently injuring his hand in a sad, ironic parallel of Tyr's own sacrifice. Only when Gorne learned that Penelope had been revived by a powerful cleric did his fury finally subside. And the realization of what he'd done crushed him.

Unable to cope with his guilt and shame, Gorne found himself cut off from most of his divine powers. His overwhelming self-loathing created a sort of mental block between himself and the divine. Although his parents and Penelope unconditionally love and forgive him, and even with Tyr himself willing to restore the guilt-ridden paladin, Gorne's own conviction that he is unworthy became a self-fulfilling prophecy, weakening his divine connection greatly.

So, he left Fort Defiance and Moonhunter Valley to travel Faerûn as a true paladin, dedicated to the pursuit of helping others. In a world where thousands of innocents are plagued by malevolent threats, he hopes that if he helps enough people, restores enough order, rights enough wrongs, he will be able to rid himself of the crippling guilt he feels for succumbing to the Blood of Gruumsh and hurting those he loved most.

Relationships

Throughout his life, Gorne has formed numerous significant bonds that have shaped who he is. From his unusual parentage to his tragic marriage, these relationships define much of his character and motivations.

Sir Aldren Kevlor Father

A human paladin of Tyr and knight of the Order of the Gauntlet. Aldren was assigned to rebuild and command Fort Defiance, securing the High Road trade route near Neverwinter. His unusual alliance with the Moonhunters tribe led to his marriage with Varka and eventually the birth of Gorne.

Aldren taught Gorne swordsmanship, combat discipline, and the principles of justice that eventually led him to become a paladin of Tyr. Their relationship has always remained strong and steadfast, even when Gorne, consumed by vengeance, injured his father's hand during his rampage against the Cragmaw goblins, a wound that mirrors Tyr's own sacrifice.

"A true paladin judges by action, not birth. Remember that, my son, when others judge you by your tusks rather than your heart." — Sir Aldren Kevlor

Varka Mother

Chieftain of the Moonhunters tribe, an unusual orcish community that rejected the violent ways of Gruumsh in favor of the more balanced teachings of Silvanus and Selûne. Varka is a skilled warrior and wise leader who guided her people away from their savage origins.

She taught Gorne to control the "Blood of Gruumsh" - the dangerous rage inherent to orcish blood - and to embrace both sides of his heritage. Her lessons about restraint and balance made a profound impact on him, making his fall to vengeance all the more devastating.

"The Blood calls to all of us, Gorne; it always has. But it is up to us to stifle it and choose not to answer." — Varka of the Moonhunters

Penelope Wife

A half-elven bard and diplomat who worked under Lord Neverember in Neverwinter, Penelope was secretly an agent of the Harpers, gathering intelligence to ensure the city's rebuilding served the common good. Like many Harpers, she used her musical talents to gather information while appearing to simply entertain. She met Gorne during a diplomatic summit in Neverwinter and was drawn to his unique perspective as a half-orc raised between two cultures.

Their marriage was one of Gorne's happiest moments in his life. Her death at the hands of Cragmaw goblins triggered his fall from grace, though her miraculous revival by a powerful cleric ultimately stopped his rampage. In the aftermath, Penelope fully supported Gorne's decision to seek redemption through travel. Understanding his need for atonement, she writes to him often from Neverwinter where she continues her work for both the city and the Harpers.

"Careful, Gorne. Smile at me like that again and I might forget we're in public." — Penelope

Stormfang Spirit Guide

A large gray wolf that mysteriously appears at significant moments in Gorne's life. According to Moonhunter shamans, Stormfang is a messenger of Silvanus, the Forest Father, marking Gorne as one who walks between worlds.

The wolf typically appears during moments of danger or important decisions, and vanishes just as mysteriously. Since Gorne's fall and separation from Tyr's grace, Stormfang has not been seen, though Gorne sometimes dreams of the wolf leading him through dark forests toward a distant light.

King Grol Enemy

The bugbear leader of the Cragmaw tribe whose goblins killed Penelope. Though Gorne's vengeful rampage decimated much of the tribe, King Grol escaped and has rebuilt a portion of his forces. He now rules from the reclaimed Cragmaw Castle deep in Neverwinter Wood, nursing a burning hatred for the "Tusked Demon" who nearly destroyed his tribe.

Survivors tell of Grol's obsession with revenge, collecting weapons and allies in preparation for the day he can return the slaughter visited upon his people. The memory of noncombatants and children killed during his fury haunts Gorne, but he remains unaware that his greatest enemy not only lives, but plots his destruction with single-minded purpose.

Significant Locations

Several places have played important roles in Gorne's life, from his unusual birthplace to the sites of his greatest triumphs and failures.

Fort Defiance

▼

History

Fort Defiance was originally an abandoned outpost along the High Road, reclaimed by Sir Aldren Kevlor and a small contingent of knights from the Order of the Gauntlet. The fort was named for both its decrepit initial condition and the determination of its defenders to hold it against all odds.

When Sir Aldren encountered the Moonhunters tribe and formed an alliance with their leader Varka, the fort became a unique experiment in human-orc cooperation. Despite skepticism from authorities in Neverwinter and resistance within the Order itself, the arrangement proved successful, with the fort securing a significant portion of the High Road from bandit attacks and monster incursions.

Current Status

Today, Fort Defiance serves as both a military garrison and a trading post where merchants traveling the High Road can find protection, supplies, and occasionally unusual goods crafted by the Moonhunters. The fort's reputation has grown in recent years, and it now stands as a symbol of potential cooperation between races often at odds.

Since Gorne's departure on his quest for redemption, leadership of the fort has fallen entirely to Sir Aldren, though his hand injury limits his combat effectiveness. The knights and Moonhunter warriors continue to patrol the High Road together, maintaining the unusual alliance.

Notable Locations

  • The Great Hall: Central meeting place and dining hall, where knights and Moonhunters gather for meals and councils
  • The Watchtower: Highest point in the fort, offering views of the surrounding countryside and the High Road in both directions
  • The Shrine of Two Moons: A small sanctuary where followers of both Tyr and Selûne can pray and meditate
  • The Training Yard: Where Gorne spent much of his youth learning swordsmanship from his father and control from his mother

Moonhunter Valley

▼

Geography

Moonhunter Valley is a hidden valley within Neverwinter Wood, approximately a half-day's journey from Fort Defiance. Protected by steep slopes and dense forest growth, the valley remains difficult to find without a guide. A clear stream flows through its center, widening into a small lake at the valley's heart.

The valley's eastern entrance narrows to a defensible gap barely thirty yards across, making it ideal for the Moonhunters who sought safety from traditional orc tribes that viewed them as heretics. The valley's fertile soil supports abundant plant life, including several groves of ancient oak trees considered sacred to Silvanus.

The Settlement

Unlike the crude camps of traditional orc tribes, the Moonhunter settlement features permanent structures built to last generations. These include longhouses for families, a central meeting hall, workshops for artisans, and a circular stone platform where rituals to Silvanus and Selûne are performed under the open sky.

The Moonhunters practice sustainable living, hunting only what they need and cultivating gardens of medicinal herbs and edible plants. They have established a harmonious relationship with the valley's ecosystem, viewing themselves as guardians rather than masters of this natural sanctuary.

Significance to Gorne

Gorne spent much of his childhood in Moonhunter Valley, learning the tribe's practices for controlling the "Blood of Gruumsh" and developing an appreciation for the balance of nature that Silvanus represents. It was here that he first encountered Stormfang, the mysterious wolf that the tribe's shamans identified as a messenger of Silvanus.

The valley remains a place of safety for the Moonhunters, though they maintain strong ties with Fort Defiance. Varka continues to lead the tribe, though she often travels between the valley and the fort, maintaining the alliance her relationship with Aldren created.

Neverwinter

▼

The City of Skilled Hands

Neverwinter, also known as the City of Skilled Hands or the Jewel of the North, is one of the most prominent cities on the Sword Coast. Named for the Neverwinter River that flows through it - a river that mysteriously never freezes despite the cold northern climate - the city has long been known for its master craftsmen, beautiful gardens, and colored glass lamps.

In 1451 DR, the city was devastated by the eruption of Mount Hotenow, which destroyed much of its infrastructure and killed many of its citizens. Since then, Lord Dagult Neverember has led reconstruction efforts, declaring himself Lord Protector of Neverwinter and investing considerable resources in rebuilding the city.

Current Status

By 1489 DR, Neverwinter has partially recovered from the cataclysm. The city walls have been restored, and the population has begun to grow again as merchants, craftspeople, and opportunists arrive hoping to make their fortunes in the rebuilding city. However, many districts remain in ruins, and dangers still lurk in the less-controlled areas.

Lord Neverember maintains control through a combination of political maneuvering and military force, with his mercenary army keeping order while reconstruction continues. The rebuilding of Neverwinter is a priority for the Lords' Alliance, as the city's strategic location makes it vital for trade along the Sword Coast.

Connection to Gorne

Gorne visited Neverwinter occasionally during his youth, accompanying his father on official business or his mother on trading missions. It was in Neverwinter that he met Penelope, who served in Lord Neverember's diplomatic corps while secretly gathering information for the Harpers.

Following their marriage, Gorne and Penelope maintained a small apartment in the Blacklake District, though they spent most of their time at Fort Defiance. After Penelope's death and revival, she returned to Neverwinter to recover while Gorne embarked on his quest for redemption.

Neverwinter Wood

▼

Ancient Forest

Neverwinter Wood is a vast, ancient forest east of the city of Neverwinter. It is one of the largest forests in the North, stretching for miles in all directions and harboring many secrets and dangers. The forest is dense and often misty, with towering trees that have stood for centuries.

The wood has a reputation for being haunted or enchanted, with local folklore warning of strange lights, mysterious voices, and travelers who enter never to return. While some of these tales are mere superstition, the forest does indeed contain powerful magic and ancient ruins dating back to fallen civilizations.

Inhabitants

Neverwinter Wood is home to a diverse array of creatures and communities. Wolves, bears, and other natural wildlife abound, alongside more magical creatures like dryads and treants who protect the oldest groves. Various tribes of goblins, including the Cragmaws, make their lairs in caves and ruined structures throughout the forest.

The northwestern region of the wood contains Moonhunter Valley, home to the unusual orc tribe that raised Gorne. Deeper in the forest, more dangerous predators lurk, including displacer beasts, owlbears, and occasionally more sinister entities that have escaped notice or capture.

Significance to Gorne

Gorne knows Neverwinter Wood better than most humans, having explored it extensively during his childhood with his mother's Moonhunter tribemates. He learned to hunt, track, and navigate through the dense forest, developing skills that would later serve him well as a paladin.

The wood is also the site of his greatest shame - it was within these trees that he hunted and slayed the Cragmaw goblins that killed his wife Penelope, and where Gorne further unleashed his vengeful fury upon the entire tribe. The memories of what happened there continue to haunt him during his quest for redemption.

The High Road

▼

Major Trade Route

The High Road is one of the most important trade routes along the Sword Coast, running north from Waterdeep along the coast through Neverwinter and eventually to Luskan. For centuries, it has served as the primary artery for commerce and travel in the region, connecting major cities and smaller settlements alike.

Portions of the road were damaged during the cataclysm that struck Neverwinter, and in the resulting chaos, several sections became dangerous as monsters and bandits moved into the area. Restoring safe travel along the High Road has become a priority for both Waterdeep and Neverwinter, as commerce is vital to the region's recovery.

Fort Defiance's Role

Lord Neverember assigned the Order of the Gauntlet the task of securing the northern section of the High Road, providing a safe corridor for trade to and from Neverwinter. Fort Defiance was established as a key garrison in this effort, positioned to patrol a twenty-mile stretch of the road that had become particularly notorious for bandit attacks.

Under Sir Aldren's leadership and with the assistance of the Moonhunters tribe, this section of the High Road has become significantly safer in recent years. Merchant caravans now regularly travel with smaller guards than previously required, and some even choose to spend nights at Fort Defiance rather than risk camping in the open.

Current Threats

Despite the improvements, the High Road still faces various threats. Bandits continue to operate in less-patrolled areas, and the Cragmaw goblins occasionally venture from Neverwinter Wood to attack isolated travelers. Creatures displaced by the cataclysm sometimes wander onto the road, creating danger for the unprepared.

More worrying are rumors of organized criminal elements from Luskan seeking to establish a presence along the road, potentially complicating the straightforward security efforts of Fort Defiance. Lord Neverember has promised additional resources to counter this threat, though such support has been slow to materialize.

Factions

Various organizations have influenced Gorne's life and continue to shape his journey. Some gave him purpose, others taught him valuable lessons, and some became bitter enemies.

Order of the Gauntlet

A coalition of righteous warriors, paladins and clerics who dedicated themselves to protecting the innocent and fighting evil across Faerûn. Founded in the 15th century DR, the Order emphasized proactive vigilance against threats while maintaining a strong moral code.

Sir Aldren Kevlor was a Knight-Lieutenant in the Order, assigned to secure the High Road near Neverwinter by rebuilding and commanding Fort Defiance. The Order initially opposed his alliance with the Moonhunters tribe but eventually accepted it as a practical measure to secure the region.

While Gorne was never formally inducted into the Order, he was raised with its principles and many members served as mentors during his youth. After his fall from grace, his relationship with the Order became strained, with some members viewing him as a cautionary tale about the dangers of unchecked emotion.

Moonhunters Tribe

An unusual tribe of orcs who rejected the violent teachings of Gruumsh in favor of the more balanced path of Silvanus and Selûne. Founded generations ago by a visionary chieftain named Grohk Steelclaw who renounced the orcish pantheon after witnessing how its endless calls for violence threatened to destroy his people.

Led by Varka, Gorne's mother, the Moonhunters established a settlement in a hidden valley near Fort Defiance. Their alliance with Sir Aldren's forces proved mutually beneficial, with the orcs providing superior knowledge of the local wilderness while gaining protection from more traditional orc tribes that viewed them as heretics.

The Moonhunters place great emphasis on controlling what they call the "Blood of Gruumsh" - the rage inherent to orcish nature. Their shamans teach meditation techniques and rituals to master this dangerous fury, making Gorne's eventual fall to vengeful rage all the more tragic in their eyes.

The Harpers

A semi-secret organization dedicated to preserving knowledge, maintaining balance, and opposing tyranny across Faerûn. Operating through a loose network of individual agents rather than a rigid hierarchy, the Harpers gather information and work subtly to promote fairness and freedom.

Penelope, Gorne's wife, was secretly a Harper agent assigned to monitor the rebuilding of Neverwinter under Lord Neverember. Her diplomatic position provided excellent cover for her true mission: ensuring the city's restoration served all its people, not just the wealthy and powerful.

While Gorne was never directly involved with the Harpers, he learned much about their ideals from Penelope. After her death and revival, some Harper agents have kept a watchful eye on him, concerned about his potential for both great good and terrible destruction.

Cragmaw Tribe

A tribe of goblins, hobgoblins and bugbears operating in Neverwinter Wood during the late 15th century DR. Named for their practice of sharpening their teeth to appear more ferocious, the Cragmaw were led by King Grol from their stronghold in Cragmaw Castle.

The Cragmaws became Gorne's bitter enemies after they attacked and killed his wife Penelope during an ambush on the forest paths near Neverwinter. This event triggered his embrace of Hoar, god of vengeance, and his subsequent rampage that nearly destroyed the entire tribe.

While most of the Cragmaws were killed during Gorne's vengeful assault, some survivors escaped into the deeper parts of Neverwinter Wood. These remnants tell fearful stories of the "Tusked Demon" whose fury was unlike anything they had ever witnessed.

Lord's Alliance

A coalition of political powers throughout the North, dedicated to mutual security and prosperity. Member cities include Waterdeep, Neverwinter, Baldur's Gate, and Silverymoon, among others, each maintaining a degree of independence while cooperating on matters of common interest.

Lord Dagult Neverember, who led the reconstruction of Neverwinter after the eruption of Mount Hotenow, represented the city in the Alliance. His diplomatic corps, including Penelope, worked to secure trade agreements and military aid to support Neverwinter's rebuilding efforts.

Gorne's connection to the Alliance was primarily through Penelope and the cooperation between Neverwinter and Fort Defiance. The Alliance's officials have mixed opinions about him - some see his unique heritage as potentially valuable for diplomacy between human and orc communities, while others view his fall from grace as evidence that orcs cannot truly overcome their savage nature.

Faith & Divine Connections

Gorne's life has been deeply influenced by various deities, from his dedication to Tyr's justice to his temporary embrace of Hoar's vengeance. His mixed heritage also connects him to both human and orcish religious traditions.

Tyr

God of Justice

The Maimed God, Tyr is the lawful good deity of justice in the Forgotten Realms. He lost his right hand to Kezef the Chaos Hound and was blinded by Ao during the Time of Troubles. His followers seek balanced justice and fairness for all.

Gorne dedicated himself to Tyr at age fifteen, becoming one of the few half-orc paladins in the Realms. His connection to the god was severed when he embraced vengeance against the Cragmaw tribe. Though Tyr would welcome him back, Gorne's own shame blocks his reconnection to the deity's grace.

Hoar

God of Vengeance

The Doombringer, Hoar is the deity of retribution invoked by those seeking to repay an eye for an eye. Unlike Tyr's balanced approach to justice, Hoar embodies bitter revenge and poetic punishment for wrongdoers.

After Penelope's death, Gorne temporarily embraced Hoar's vengeful aspects, allowing the god's influence to fuel his rage against the Cragmaw tribe. The cruelty he exhibited during this rampage still haunts him, and he has since rejected this connection entirely.

Silvanus

God of Wild Nature

The Oak Father, Silvanus is one of the oldest deities in Faerûn, representing wild nature and the balance of natural forces. His philosophy emphasizes that wilderness is the true state of the world, to be preserved and respected.

Through his mother's tribe, Gorne learned reverence for Silvanus. The mysterious wolf Stormfang is believed to be a messenger of the Oak Father, marking Gorne for an important role in maintaining balance. This connection remains strong despite his fall from Tyr's grace.

Selûne

Goddess of the Moon

The Moonmaiden, Selûne is the goddess of the moon, stars, navigation, and travelers. She embodies light in darkness and guidance through difficult passages.

Along with Silvanus, Selûne is one of the patron deities of the Moonhunters tribe. Her teachings about finding one's way through darkness hold special meaning for Gorne in his current quest for redemption, and he often finds himself praying to her during his travels.

Gruumsh

God of Orcs

One-Eye, Gruumsh is the chief deity of the orcish pantheon who demands constant warfare and conquest. The Moonhunters tribe explicitly rejected his violent teachings generations ago.

Though Gorne has never worshipped Gruumsh, the "Blood of Gruumsh" - the rage inherent to orcish nature - flows in his veins. His temporary surrender to this rage during his vengeful rampage deeply shamed him, reinforcing his commitment to the more balanced path taught by his mother.

The Blood of Gruumsh

A central concept in Gorne's story is the "Blood of Gruumsh" - the innate rage and destructive fury that orcs believe they inherit from their chief deity. This primal anger provides tremendous strength in battle but can lead to uncontrolled violence if not properly mastered.

The Moonhunters tribe developed specific practices to control this dangerous aspect of their nature, rejecting Gruumsh's call to endless violence in favor of Silvanus's more balanced approach. Varka taught these techniques to her son from an early age, helping him understand and control the fierce emotions within him.

When Gorne surrendered to vengeance after Penelope's death, he essentially gave in to the Blood of Gruumsh, allowing the destructive rage to overwhelm his training and discipline. The aftermath of this surrender - the innocent lives he took, the injury to his father - represents his greatest shame and the primary obstacle to reclaiming his divine connection to Tyr.

"The Blood makes us strong, but control makes us powerful. Any beast can destroy; wisdom lies in knowing when not to." — Teaching of the Moonhunter shamans
